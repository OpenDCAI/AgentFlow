# Phase 1: Environment Exploration Guide
* **Exploration Strategy**:
  - **Prioritize recent, cutoff-resistant papers.** During exploration, choose papers that are recent enough that a base model is less likely to answer from memorized training data alone. Then verify that the decisive evidence is not trivially present in the abstract. This keeps the task focused on retrieval and reading rather than latent recall.
  - **Search at the paper level, then gather at the section level.** First identify likely candidate papers, and then inspect sections, figures, methods, or results paragraphs that directly answer the question. Do not stop at title or abstract matching. Full-text literature synthesis depends on this two-stage exploration pattern.
  - **Record evidence provenance tightly.** For each retained evidence chunk, log the paper identity and the local section or evidence summary that supports the answer. This provenance is essential because literature questions often feel plausible even when sourced from the wrong paper. Tight provenance reduces false confidence.
  - **Probe calibrated uncertainty.** Explore at least some queries where retrieval may fail or the gathered evidence remains incomplete, and note whether the agent should answer or say it cannot tell. Literature RAG is valuable partly because it can refuse rather than hallucinate. A strong skill must model both successful synthesis and bounded uncertainty.
* **Target Trajectory Profile**:
  - **Paper discovery precedes evidence extraction.** A good trajectory separates finding candidate papers from extracting the decisive evidence passage. This helps diagnose whether a failure came from paper selection or from intra-paper reading. Literature tasks often fail at one of these two stages, and the trajectory should reveal which.
  - **Use multi-pass search when needed.** If the first search query is weak, the trajectory should refine keywords, synonyms, or subquestions before giving up. Scientific wording is often specialized, so one query rarely suffices. Iterative search is therefore a normal part of the desired behavior.
  - **Evidence summaries remain question-conditioned.** When summarizing a paper section, the trace should state why that section matters for the specific question instead of producing a generic abstract. This keeps the context library lean and task-directed. Generic paper summaries are too lossy for precise QA.
  - **Final answer is cited and calibrated.** The answer step should either provide the conclusion with source support or explicitly state that the evidence is insufficient. Do not fill gaps with domain-sounding speculation. Calibration is part of the capability, not an optional extra.
