# Phase 1: Environment Exploration Guide
* **Exploration Strategy**:
  - **Construct near-miss distractor pools.** Start from one answer-bearing seed passage and deliberately retrieve additional passages that share the same entity type, topic, and lexical field but omit the decisive fact. Generate year-adjacent, sibling-entity, or same-event distractors, then keep only distractors that remain plausible after a quick skim. For example, a question about a 2022 winner should be surrounded by 2021 winner passages rather than unrelated culture news.
  - **Control the noise ratio.** Vary the number and placement of distractors so the task does not collapse into trivial retrieval or impossible clutter. Create several exploration traces with low, medium, and high distractor density, and record exactly which chunk first makes the trajectory fragile. An edge case is a bundle where every passage mentions the same prize or metric but only one passage contains the correct year or value.
  - **Log why each passage is insufficient or sufficient.** Do not merely mark passages as relevant or irrelevant; record the specific field, sentence, or missing attribute that makes them decisive or non-decisive. During exploration, write one short evidence note per chunk such as 'same event but wrong year' or 'same company but wrong quarter'. This makes later synthesis precise and prevents accidental reuse of soft matches as support.
  - **Stress-test surface shortcuts.** Probe whether the model can still succeed when the answer-bearing passage is shorter, later in the list, or lexically weaker than the distractors. Run at least one exploration variant where distractors are more verbose or more prominently placed than the correct passage. A common failure case is that the model trusts the longest or earliest passage instead of the most specific one.
* **Target Trajectory Profile**:
  - **Evidence-accounting first.** A good trajectory explicitly accounts for each retrieved passage before answering, rather than jumping from the first topical match to a conclusion. The agent should note which chunk contains the decisive slot and which chunks are only background or distractors. If the trace cannot explain why each major distractor was discarded, the trajectory is too weak for this skill.
  - **High-specificity grounding.** The retained evidence should contain the exact discriminating attribute such as the target year, value, or role label. Require the trace to preserve the smallest sufficient span, not a vague paragraph-level summary. For example, 'literature prize passage' is not enough if the real discriminator is '2022'.
  - **Minimal reliance on paraphrase-only support.** The trajectory should not depend on paraphrasing that washes away the crucial difference between correct evidence and noise. When summarizing, keep the differentiating token visible in the trace so reviewers can verify the step. Otherwise a 2021/2022 distinction, or Q3/Q4 distinction, is easily lost.
  - **Noise-aware finalization.** The answer step should explicitly state that the discarded passages are related but non-answer-bearing. This makes the final data teach the downstream model that topical similarity is not enough for support. An edge case is when the distractor is factually true in general but irrelevant to the asked slice of the question.
