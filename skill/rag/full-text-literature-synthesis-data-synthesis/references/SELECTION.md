# Phase 2: Trajectory Selection Criteria
* **Acceptance Metrics**:
  - **Retrieval from full text is actually necessary.** Accept only when the answer cannot be reliably recovered from title-level or abstract-level cues alone. The case should force the agent into full-text evidence gathering. Otherwise the skill degenerates into metadata search.
  - **A stable, supportable answer exists.** Accept only if a reviewer can point to a bounded piece of paper evidence that settles the answer or justifies an explicit 'cannot answer'. Scientific ambiguity is common, but the benchmark sample itself must remain scorable. Stable support matters more than breadth.
  - **Question targets genuine literature content.** The accepted sample should reflect a real scientific finding, comparison, or result rather than a generic textbook fact. This is what distinguishes literature synthesis from ordinary QA. Recent or specialist findings are especially valuable.
  - **Calibration pathway is available.** Accept only if the trajectory has a principled way to say unsure when search or evidence gathering fails. This keeps the skill aligned with literature-review behavior rather than answer-by-default behavior. Scientific RAG must preserve knowledge boundaries.
* **Rejection Criteria**:
  - **Abstract-only shortcut exists.** Reject cases where the answer is directly recoverable from the abstract or the paper title. Those are too weak for full-text synthesis training. The full-text requirement should be real.
  - **Paper provenance is unstable.** Reject if several near-duplicate papers or review articles could all appear correct and the trace cannot cleanly justify one support set. Literature synthesis needs crisp provenance. Ambiguous support sets encourage citation noise.
  - **Question is pure domain trivia.** Reject if the query could be answered from broad background knowledge without retrieving the target paper. The skill is about evidence-backed literature use, not general biomedical recall. Recentness and specificity are the best filters here.
  - **Final answer requires heavy external experimentation or unseen figure interpretation.** Reject cases where the decisive evidence cannot be represented in text or straightforward extracted passages for a text-first RAG agent. The target agent is read-only over textual or structured knowledge. Stay within what the environment can expose faithfully.
